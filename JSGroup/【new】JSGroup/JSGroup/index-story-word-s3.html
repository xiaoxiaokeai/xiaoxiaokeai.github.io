Once there was a kind-hearted aristocrat, his wife died of illness, leaving him and his three daughters. The aristocrat tried
many inventions and failed, but he also ran out of money, so they had to move to a farmhouse, and his daughters had to
cook, sew and clean themselves.

A few years later, the daughters were getting married, and the father became more depressed because he had no money to
buy dowry for her daughters.

One night, after the girls washed their clothes, they hung the stockings in front of the fireplace and dried them. The sage
Nicholas came to their house that night after knowing their father's situation. He saw from the window that the family had
fallen asleep and also noticed the stockings of the girls. Then he took out three small bags of gold from his pocket and
threw it down the chimney, just falling into the girls' stockings.

On the second morning, the daughters woke up to find their stockings filled with gold enough for them to buy dowry. This
aristocrat can also see his daughters marry and live happily ever after.

Later, children from all over the world inherited the tradition of hanging Christmas stockings. In some countries, children
have other similar customs, such as in France, children put their shoes near the fireplace and so on.
